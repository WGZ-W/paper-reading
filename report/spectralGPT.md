

[1]. D. Hong et al., "SpectralGPT: Spectral Remote Sensing Foundation Model," in IEEE Transactions on Pattern Analysis and Machine Intelligence, vol. 46, no. 8, pp. 5227-5244, Aug. 2024, doi: 10.1109/TPAMI.2024.3362475.



This paper proposed a foundation model, named SpectralGPT, to process spectral data.
Compared to existing foundation models, SpectralGPT has four advantages.

- diverse input in terms of size, resolution ...
- multi-target reconstruction
- extract knowledge from spatial-spectral mixed information
- over 600 million parameters

With the develop of satellite missions, spectral data have a rapid expansion, but challenging also coming.
- Extraction and mining capabilities from spectral data.
- Limited pridiction and interpretation abilities for downstream tasks on a few label cases.

The development of foundation models based on pretraining techniques and self-supervised learning and transformer methods.  

Pretraining agent tasks usually divided into contrastive learning and generative learning.  

Contrastive learning has two representative frameworks, momentum contrast and simple contrastive learning.  

Generative learning also has two methods, masked image modeling(MIM), and masked autoencoders(MAE).  

Existing RS foundation models and foundation models for video data is not well-suited for spectral data.
